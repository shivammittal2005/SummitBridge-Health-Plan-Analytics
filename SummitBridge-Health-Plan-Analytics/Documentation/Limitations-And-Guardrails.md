# Limitations and Guardrails

## Analytical Limitations

- The 800-member sample is not the full enterprise population.
- Claim lines were treated as visits because that was the case definition.
- A production system should group claim lines into encounter episodes.
- Diagnosis-based ED categories cannot replace clinical triage.
- High-cost-member performance may be affected by regression to the mean.
- Retention analysis identifies associations, not causality.
- External benchmarks are directional and should be revalidated before use.

## Financial Limitations

- Scenario estimates are gross, not net.
- Program cost, provider capacity, member adoption and operational constraints are excluded.
- Retention value is not monetized.
- The $1.2M scenario uses stronger assumptions and is illustrative.

## Quality Guardrails

Track:

- ED revisit rate
- 30-day readmissions
- Urgent-care conversion
- PCP follow-up
- Medication adherence
- Member complaints
- Repeat admissions
- Member retention

A pilot should be modified or stopped if quality deteriorates, even when gross cost estimates appear favorable.
