# KPI Definitions

| KPI | Definition | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| PMPM | Total allowed spend divided by member months | Normalizes medical cost for enrollment exposure |
| ED Claim Lines per 1,000 | ED claim lines divided by member months, multiplied by 1,000 | Tracks emergency utilization intensity |
| High-Cost Concentration | Allowed spend generated by the selected high-cost cohort divided by total allowed spend | Measures member-level cost concentration |
| Inpatient Allowed Spend | Sum of allowed amount for inpatient claims | Tracks the largest cost pool |
| Specialty Rx Allowed Spend | Sum of allowed amount for Specialty Rx claims | Monitors concentrated pharmacy cost |
| OON Leakage Rate | Out-of-network allowed spend divided by total allowed spend | Measures network leakage |
| Churn Rate | Disenrolled members divided by enrolled members | Tracks retention |
| PCP 7-Day Follow-Up | Eligible members receiving PCP follow-up within seven days | Quality and access indicator |
| Urgent-Care Conversion | Clinically appropriate ED candidates routed to urgent care | Pilot adoption metric |
| 30-Day Readmission Rate | Readmissions within 30 days divided by eligible discharges | Quality guardrail |
| Medication Adherence | Members meeting the defined adherence threshold | Pharmacy quality guardrail |
| Member Complaints | Complaints associated with the intervention population | Experience guardrail |

## Interpretation Rules

- Financial metrics should be paired with quality guardrails.
- A decrease in cost is not considered successful when readmissions, revisits, adherence or complaints deteriorate.
- Retention metrics should not be interpreted causally without a controlled pilot.
